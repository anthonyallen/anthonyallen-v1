About

What is The Store Menangle?

A café and boutique grocer in a 1904 heritage building. The same building also houses the Menangle Post Office.

What are the opening hours?

Monday 6:30am–3pm. Tuesday to Friday 7am–2pm. Saturday and Sunday 8am–2pm.

Is it in a heritage building?

Yes. The 1904 building was designed by Sydney architecture firm Sulman and Power. Over the decades it’s been a barn, function space, accommodation, general store, bottle shop and post office. Today it houses the café, the Menangle Post Office, and the original bakery ovens are still in place.

What coffee and bread do they serve?

Mecca coffee, Sonoma bread and pastries, Cedar Creek Cider, and a regular rotation of local produce.

Do they sell groceries?

Yes. Alongside the café menu you’ll find Sonoma bread and pastries, jams, pickles, cold meats, fresh flowers, Cedar Creek Cider, and a regular rotation of local produce and pantry staples.

Is there a post office on site?

Yes. The Menangle Post Office operates from the same building, open Monday to Friday 9am–5pm. The phone number is shared with the café: (02) 4633 8101.

How do I get in touch?

Phone (02) 4633 8101 or email admin@thestoremenangle.com.au. For bookings or function enquiries, call the café directly.

Is it accessible?

Yes. Destination NSW lists The Store as actively welcoming people with access needs.

Is this the official website?

No. The Store doesn’t have one, so this page fills the gap. Menu and prices come from the café’s Google Maps listing and other published sources, updated periodically.
